 I made Kollaj with the intention to wake a more artistically oriented mindset breaking nowadays's ''in the box'' streamlined fashion of content sharing.
That is, all content we shared untill now was made to fit a certain layout, order. Not only in design terms, but also as a piece. Kollaj aims to break this rule, it is a tool that allows you, and everyone, to gather the important pieces, and conjugate them into something unique. Be it to make a piece of art, to share how your day or vacations followed, or keep a kollaj with the latest news.
Allowing you to cut photos (and later on videos), is a way to make your feed personal, and pleasant. The geometric patterns you will use in your Kollaj will form an unique and natural feel of your feed.

And YES! Cutting is really easy, the Editor, Ed for short is about a couple of months worth of work, and he came from Coffeelandia. Ed is the core of Kollaj. He had various shapes, and forms, but he finally materialized in the island of stability.
Both in terms of feel, and being stable enough to release.
His final accounted shape can be found in the source code of Kollaj and the base model @ my Codepen account on codepen.io/spacewalkingninja.
Ed works with snapsvg under the hood, and allows you to make two consecutive cuts to the photo. Not more, not less.
The side of the photo that gets deleted is the smaller, NOT containing the center point.. yeah that's why it appears when you cut.
This is about the point where I mention Ed's worst nightmare. New Phones on Kollaj.
Not that he has some kind of problem with them or something, it's just that as the feed os the user expands, I think this is HIGHLY LIKELY to be due to either CSS Pixels, to Pixels, and about I don't know what other kinds of conversion and voodoo, values get distorted to about 0.01-0.09 % over a greater distance from the start when saving the photo. Like when saving a photo at 6 000 pixels from the start, this picture might get saved @what is visually 6090px.
This is why there is the calibration tool at your disposal, when things start going wrong. Usually the blunt test tells you how well your phone is calibrated. (Cut a blunt, and save it over the mouth of someone on a picture far down your Kollaj, if the blunt stays where you put it, EVERYTHING IS FINE! If not satisfied, calibrate, higher calibration means photos will appear HIGHER on your Kollaj.)
This is one of my most prioritized bugs to be resolved. Any ideas are appreaciated and in the meantime, as soon as I have time to really fix this, I shall :) !

Only you are allowed to see the medium color of your photos. You can't see other users' photos color, or vice-versa.
